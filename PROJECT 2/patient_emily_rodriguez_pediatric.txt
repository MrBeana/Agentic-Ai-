Diagnosis:
  1. Moderate Persistent Asthma (newly diagnosed)
     - Daytime symptoms >1 day/week, nighttime symptoms >1 night/week
     - SpO2 94%, FEV1 72%, significant bronchodilator reversibility
  2. Allergic sensitisation (house dust mites, pollen)
  3. Atopic dermatitis (pre-existing, mild)
  4. Allergic rhinitis (pre-existing, controlled)

Asthma Action Plan:
  Green Zone (Well controlled):
    - Fluticasone 88mcg/puff — 2 puffs twice daily (controller)
    - Montelukast 5mg once daily (leukotriene modifier — also helps rhinitis)
    - Continue Cetirizine during allergy season

  Yellow Zone (Worsening symptoms):
    - Albuterol (Salbutamol) MDI 90mcg — 2–4 puffs every 4–6 hours PRN
    - If no improvement in 24h or SpO2 <92%, seek urgent care

  Red Zone (Emergency):
    - Continuous albuterol via nebuliser
    - Call 911 or go to ER immediately if severe difficulty breathing,
      cyanosis, or unable to speak in full sentences

Environmental Recommendations:
  - Encase mattress and pillows in allergen-proof covers
  - Wash bedding in hot water (≥130°F / 54°C) weekly
  - Remove bedroom carpet; replace with hard flooring
  - Use HEPA air purifier in bedroom
  - Keep windows closed during high pollen days
  - No tobacco smoke exposure (counsel parents)
  - Avoid known triggers during exercise (consider pre-treatment with albuterol
    15 minutes before exercise)

Follow-up:
  - 2 weeks: Reassess inhaler technique and symptom control
  - 6 weeks: Spirometry repeat to assess controller therapy response
  - Refer to Pediatric Allergy clinic for consideration of allergen immunotherapy

Precautions for Parents & School:
  - Provide school nurse with copy of Asthma Action Plan
  - Ensure Albuterol inhaler is kept at school
  - Inform PE teacher of diagnosis — Emily may need to pre-medicate before PE class
  - Do not administer aspirin or NSAIDs (may worsen asthma in sensitive individuals)
  - Watch for signs of anaphylaxis if new foods/exposures occur (carry antihistamine)

Prognosis:
  Asthma in children is manageable with proper controller therapy and trigger avoidance.
  Many children show significant improvement or remission by adolescence with
  appropriate treatment. Parental education and adherence to the action plan
  are critical to good outcomes.
